# DESIGN RATIONALE

## Component Intelligence Applied

- **Typography Hierarchy**:  Headline/Large is used for the main product title to provide prominence. Title/Large is used for the price to highlight a key piece of information. Title/Medium is used for section headers (Specifications), and Body/Medium and Body/Small are used for descriptive text and specifications to maintain readability.  

- **Spacing System**:  The `itemSpacing` of 16px is used throughout the main content area to provide visual separation between different sections.  Padding is used within containers to create visual breathing room around content.

- **Component Behavior**: The `IMAGES` component is used for the product gallery, allowing for a carousel of images.  The `Button` component is used for both primary and secondary actions, with appropriate size and type variants to reflect their importance.  The `Badge` component is leveraged for "Last Updated" and is assumed to work.


- **Color Application**: Primary/primary50 (green) is used for the price to associate it with the brand and highlight its importance. Neutral/neutral90 (dark gray) is used for primary text, and Neutral/neutral50 (medium gray) is used for secondary text and labels.  This ensures clear visual hierarchy and brand consistency.

- **Layout Strategy**: The three-container architecture is strictly adhered to for top-level navigation (appbar, content, bottom navigation). The `layoutMode` is set to "VERTICAL" for the main layout and "HORIZONTAL" for the buttons, facilitating a natural top-to-bottom flow of content.

## Domain Context Integration

- **Industry Patterns**: The design incorporates common e-commerce patterns such as product galleries, pricing, detailed specifications, and clear calls to action.

- **User Mental Models**: The layout prioritizes key information (title, price, condition, and seller profile) first and foremost, ensuring fast and efficient product review.

- **Contextual Adaptations**: Component variants are adjusted according to specific requirements.  For instance, the button variants are selected based on the primary and secondary action hierarchy.
