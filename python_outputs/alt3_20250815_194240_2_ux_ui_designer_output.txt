- **Typography Hierarchy**: The design leverages a clear typography hierarchy.  "Headline/Large" is used for the main product title to ensure prominence. "Caption/Small" is used for the "Listed 3 days ago" label to give it a less prominent role.

- **Spacing System**:  The `itemSpacing` of 8 and 12 within containers ensures a clean and organized layout of information blocks.  Padding within the main content container (16px) provides breathing room around the content, creating a visually balanced feel.

- **Component Behavior**: The image carousel (`10:7816`) is used to showcase multiple product images. The "Contact Seller" button is styled as a "Filled" button due to its primary action nature, while the "Add to Watchlist" button is "Outlined" to indicate its secondary action. The app bar's back button is set to visible to allow for proper navigation.

- **Color Application**: The primary brand color (`Primary/primary50`) is used for the price to highlight its importance and increase the impact of the information. Neutral colors are used extensively for text and backgrounds to maintain readability and visual consistency.

- **Layout Strategy**: The three-container mobile app architecture (top nav, content, bottom nav) is strictly followed to ensure a consistent and user-friendly layout across all screens. A vertical layout is used to create a natural flow of information.

## Domain Context Integration

- **Industry Patterns**:  The design incorporates common e-commerce patterns such as image carousels, price highlighting, product specifications, seller profiles with ratings, and clear call-to-action buttons.

- **User Mental Models**: The layout is designed to align with users' expectations for a product detail page in an e-commerce app.  Important information such as price and condition are placed prominently, while specifications and descriptions are presented in a clear and organized manner.

- **Contextual Adaptations**: The "product card" component from the design system would not have been suitable for this page due to the amount of details. The use of native elements such as native-text elements allow the content to be properly organized in a visually appealing and logical way.
